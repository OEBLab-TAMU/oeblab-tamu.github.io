Principal Investigator

Jay Kim, PhD

Associate Professor · Department of Environmental and Occupational Health
School of Public Health · Texas A&M University

Dr. Kim’s research focuses on understanding biomechanical and physiological effects of occupational physical exposures — including whole body vibration, patient handling, and VR/AR interactions — on musculoskeletal health. His work spans laboratory-based mechanistic studies and field-based investigations, developing evidence-based solutions to reduce occupational injuries.

Dr. Kim received his PhD from the University of Washington and has been an active researcher in occupational ergonomics and biomechanics for over 15 years. Research funded by NIH, NIOSH, L&I, and industry partners.

Graduate Students & Postdocs

Yinong Chen, PhD

Post-Doctoral Researcher
Human Biomechanics · Wearable Sensors · Machine Learning

Dr. Chen's research uses computational modeling and simulation to analyze the human musculoskeletal system during dynamic occupational activities, with applications in healthcare and human-machine systems engineering. He joined the OEBLab in 2024 and received his PhD in Mechanical Engineering at Texas A&M University in 2026.

Mina Salehi

PDoctoral Student (PhD)
Human Biomechanics · Markerless Motion Capture · Machine Learning

Mina uses computer vision and machine learning to make biomechanical assessment more accessible and less intruisive. Since she joined the OEB Lab in Fall 2022 with a Provost scholarship, she had recieved multiple competitive grants and awards.

Allen Chan

Doctoral Student (PhD)
Environmental & Occupational Health · Total Worker Health

Allen approaches occupational health and safety questions from a Total Worker Health (TWH) perspective, recognizing that work is a social determinant of health and that worker health is tied to broader social determinants. He joined the OEB Lab from Fall 2022 with a Provost scholarship.
